About

I am a MolSSI Software Fellow and Postdoctoral Associate at the Rice Center for Theoretical Biological Physics who designs physics-based molecular models to reveal essential biological behaviors. I lead the development of open-source force fields like OpenAWSEM for protein dynamics, Open3SPN2 for DNA dynamics and OpenActin for modeling mesoscale cytoskeletal networks, all built in OpenMM with version control, automated testing and comprehensive documentation. I am currently working on machine-learning optimization of force field parameters with close collaboration alongside experimental labs to ensure that every model addresses real research questions. I am passionate about mentoring the next generation of scientists through programs like Science Clubs Peru, iGEM and Frontiers in Science, and I have guided both undergraduates and graduate students in hands-on biophysical software projects. I strive to deliver modular, maintainable and extensible computational frameworks that accelerate discovery, uphold reproducibility and advance an open-science community.
